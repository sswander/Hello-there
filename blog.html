Meiji shrine, Kinokuniya, Onsen

10 December 2014


So this is a condensed form of a few of my trips so far, because I didn't really have the time to update this site. I visited Meiji shrine and boy it was so stunning. The long walks of rocks and gravel road, surrounded by the yellow stretch of autumn trees... to salvation in the shrine itself. I tried to wash my hands and rinse my mouth in the well, got some omikuji and saw a shinto wedding! How lucky I was. There were also these boards where people hang their prayers on, and I thought this was especially cute.



Then the other day I also visited Shinjuku, and walked around the shopping malls and discovered Kinokuniya, the book store. Kinokuniya in Japanese looks nothing like like KINOKUNIYA. Instead it was some string of kanjis, which really made it difficult for me to recognise. But I found it, and I entered the 6-7ish storey building of knowledge and stories and enlightenments... all in Japanese. So of course I felt so outcast and I did not feel belonged at all even though I like to be surrounded by books sometimes, until I found the "foreign" section. There I found some my peace. The shelves were filled of books I understand, and some of them even really interesting.


At least they accept themselves for what they are.

Right?



Also the other day I went to Ooedo Onsen (literally big edo onsen). That was the first time I've been to any onsen and wow the experience was so amazing. I dressed in yukata and enjoyed the snacks that were sold there, and I mixed with about 40 other men in a big indoor hot spring bath. That was my first time baring myself naked in front of so many people, but strangely I didn't feel inferior or intimidated. I figured out why though: because we're all asians.


I only took a picture of the front part of the building. The rest are in videos which I won't reveal here *smugface*

I don't feel like typing anymore, so here are some random pictures from throughout the days:




Some zebra crossing. I totally forgot why I took this in the first place.




A random park at night. It was so tranquil but I couldn't take any pic without flash because it was so dark. Even with flash it still couldn't do justice to the real thing, but hey man, the camera had done its best.



That's it. Till next time!

Ueno zoo

5 December 2014


So I went to Ueno park to check out the zoo. I mean, I thought going to the zoo wasn't something that people will do when they visit Japan, so I was quite excited to be the so-called "different".

In itself though, Ueno zoo was a fun place to visit. The cold autumn breeze really made the walk enjoyable, and I saw a lot of couples, families and kids enjoying their time there. Of course I saw a lot of animals (cause hey, it's a zoo) and here are some of their pictures:


The leopard which kept moving around. I gave up taking a good shot of it.




Red panda! One of my most favourite animal in the zoo. They're small, but up close they can look majestic.




Polar bear, one of the staples in every zoo.




Water chameleon. It was genuinely hard to find at first, until it started moving and we were all like "oh hey there it is!".




The esteemed baboon which didn't even wanna look my way. Enjoy its back view.

I also saw herons and other animals I can't really remember. For most of it, I recorded videos instead of pictures, and I shall keep this blog at pictures because uploading videos takes much more work. Enjoy these meanwhile!

First night

3 December 2014


AND I've landed in Tokyo! I was so excited, recorded stuff in Narita airport and got questioned by the security guard there (laughs) I really practiced my Japanese then. I introduced myself the way I did in my Japanese classes, I laughed at myself in my mind. He let me off easy, and the trip from Narita to Tokyo (about 45 minutes with Narita express, an express train service to and fro Narita-Tokyo) and the view was so nice! I did not take pictures of it sadly. I lugged my bulky and heavy luggage (WOW NOW I KNOW WHY IT'S CALLED LUGGAGE. BECAUSE YOU HAVE TO LUG IT. GET IT?) around and find my house that I was staying at and settled down.

After that I went for dinner at CoCo Ichibanya, the curry restaurant. The staff didn't understand English at all and I felt bad for making him feel nervous by speaking in English. Luckily he understood "Menu" so it was all good, I got my tummy filled and everybody was happy. Then I wanted to take a good walk around, but apparently my camera battery died so I had to go back and replace it.

When I went back, I met a roommate from Taiwan who introduced himself as Kufer. We talked in a mix of English, Chinese and Japanese, but we understood each other. He said he was gonna find food around, and asked if I wanted to tag along and I said why not! So I did, and took a walk from Kanda to Tokyo station while talking to him about stuff (in those 3 languages). The only picture I got was this blurry building of Takashimaya, which is the first out of two times I actually saw Takashimaya in Tokyo.



Again the night walk was fun mostly because of it was so chilly. We saw tokyo station which looked so grand and pretty, and the fact that it looks so different when looked from one of the 2 sides (Marunouchi and Yaesu exits) just makes it even cooler!
